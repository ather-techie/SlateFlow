# Contributor License Agreement (CLA) — Frequently Asked Questions

## General Questions

### Q: Do I have to sign a CLA to contribute to SlateFlow?

**A:** Yes. All pull requests must have an associated CLA signature before they can be merged. The automated CLAassistant bot will check your signature on every PR.

Signing takes about **2 minutes** and requires only your name and email address.

---

### Q: Why does SlateFlow require a CLA?

**A:** The CLA serves three purposes:

1. **Legal clarity:** It explicitly documents what rights You grant SlateFlow, preventing future disputes about intellectual property ownership.

2. **Relicensing flexibility:** It allows SlateFlow to offer proprietary editions, SaaS variants, or dual-licensing arrangements without renegotiating with every contributor. This protects your investment in the Project's long-term sustainability.

3. **Contributor protection:** It clarifies that You retain copyright to Your work; SlateFlow only gets the rights You grant. You can reuse your contributions in other projects.

---

### Q: Is this CLA the same as other projects?

**A:** It's similar to CLAs used by major projects like Apache, Google, Canonical, and Facebook. Our CLA is:

- **Based on the Apache Individual CLA template** (widely recognized and battle-tested)
- **Non-assignment:** You keep your copyright
- **SaaS-friendly:** Explicitly permits proprietary and commercial relicensing
- **Contributor-friendly:** Straightforward language, minimal friction

## Copyright & Ownership Questions

### Q: If I sign the CLA, does SlateFlow own my contributions?

**A:** **No.** You retain 100% copyright ownership of your contributions. The CLA does **not** transfer ownership to SlateFlow.

What You do grant SlateFlow:
- The right to use your contributions in SlateFlow (open-source or proprietary)
- The right to sublicense your contributions under any license (MIT, commercial, SaaS, etc.)
- The right to modify and distribute your contributions

**What You retain:**
- Copyright ownership
- The right to use your contributions in your own projects
- The right to claim authorship
- The right to reuse portions in other works

---

### Q: Can I reuse my contributions in other projects?

**A:** **Yes, absolutely.** You retain full copyright ownership. You can:

- Reuse the code in your own projects
- Reuse it in other open-source projects
- License it differently elsewhere
- Claim authorship anywhere

SlateFlow's rights do not prevent You from using Your contributions however You wish.

---

## Relicensing & Dual-Licensing Questions

### Q: The CLA mentions "relicensing without restriction." What does that mean?

**A:** It means SlateFlow can:

- Change the base license of the Project (e.g., from MIT to Apache 2.0)
- Offer a proprietary, commercial, or cloud-hosted variant that incorporates your contributions
- Sublicense your contributions under multiple simultaneous licenses
- Use your contributions in products or services with different licensing

This is the core reason SlateFlow requires a CLA: to enable future business models (e.g., open-source core + commercial SaaS) without needing to renegotiate with contributors.

---

### Q: If SlateFlow offers a proprietary edition, do I get paid or credited?

**A:** SlateFlow's current CLA does **not** require revenue sharing or payment. However:

- You retain the right to credit yourself in your portfolio or resume
- The Project's contributors are publicly acknowledged in the README and release notes
- You may be acknowledged in proprietary editions at SlateFlow's discretion

If You contribute significantly and want special arrangements, discuss with the maintainers directly.

## Employer & Legal Questions

### Q: I work for a company. Can I still contribute?

**A:** **Yes, if authorized.** The CLA's employer authorization clause requires:

1. You have the authority to contribute on behalf of your employer
2. Your employer has authorized you to contribute (in writing or per your employment agreement)
3. Your contribution doesn't violate your employment agreement or employer policies

**Most tech companies allow employees to contribute to open-source projects.** Check your employee handbook or ask your manager.

---

### Q: What if my employer claims ownership of my contributions?

**A:** The CLA requires You to represent and warrant that:
- You have authority to enter into this Agreement
- Your contributions don't violate your employment agreement
- You've confirmed with your employer that You can contribute

**You are responsible for resolving any conflicts with your employer.** SlateFlow assumes Your representations are accurate.

If your employment agreement claims all your code, you may not be able to contribute unless your employer explicitly permits open-source contributions.

---

### Q: Can my company sign the CLA on my behalf?

**A:** The Individual CLA is signed by You, the contributor.

If your company wants to contribute as a legal entity or assign all your contributions to the company, a **Corporate CLA** may be available in the future. Contact the maintainers to discuss.

## Signing & Technical Questions

### Q: How do I sign the CLA?

**A:** The automated CLAassistant bot handles it:

1. **Create a pull request** to SlateFlow
2. **CLAassistant bot posts a comment** on your PR with a signing link
3. **Click the link** and fill out the form (name, email, optional GitHub OAuth)
4. **Sign electronically** — takes ~2 minutes
5. **Bot updates the PR status** to ✅ "CLA Signed"
6. **Your PR is now mergeable** (pending other checks like CI and maintainer review)

---

### Q: Do I have to sign for every contribution?

**A:** **No.** Once You've signed the CLA, it covers:

- All your past contributions to SlateFlow
- All your future contributions to SlateFlow

Your signature is stored in CLAassistant's registry under your GitHub username and email. The bot will automatically recognize you on future PRs.

---

### Q: What information do I need to sign?

**A:** You'll need:

- **Your full name** (legal name recommended, but first/last sufficient)
- **Your email address** (can be your GitHub-associated email)
- **Optional:** GitHub OAuth (simplifies sign-in; not required)

The signature is recorded in CLAassistant's service (not stored in the Git repository).

---

### Q: Is my email address or signature private?

**A:** CLAassistant maintains a private registry. Your email and name are not automatically published, but:

- Your GitHub username is public (visible on your PR)
- CLAassistant's CLA comment on the PR is public (shows ✅ "CLA Signed")
- Maintainers can query the registry to verify signatures

For full privacy details, see CLAassistant's [privacy policy](https://claassistant.io/privacy).

---

### Q: What if the CLAassistant bot doesn't comment on my PR?

**A:** This rarely happens, but possible causes:

- The bot may not be enabled for your repository yet (maintainers must configure it)
- The PR was created before the bot was enabled
- Bot is experiencing downtime

**Troubleshooting:**
1. Check that the bot has commented on other recent PRs
2. Try closing and reopening your PR
3. Mention @CLAssistant in a comment on your PR to trigger a re-check
4. Contact the maintainers if the issue persists

## What If I Disagree?

### Q: I don't want to sign a CLA. Can I still contribute?

**A:** Unfortunately, **no.** All contributions require a CLA signature before merge.

However:
- The CLA is intentionally lightweight and contributor-friendly
- Signing takes ~2 minutes
- Signing covers all your current and future contributions (one-time process)
- The CLA is based on widely-used templates (Apache, Google, etc.)

If you have specific concerns about the CLA language, contact the maintainers to discuss.

---

### Q: Can I contribute without agreeing to the relicensing clause?

**A:** **No.** The relicensing clause is non-negotiable for SlateFlow contributors.

The relicensing rights are essential for SlateFlow's ability to:
- Sustain the Project long-term via dual licensing or commercial editions
- Respond to future business needs without contributor friction
- Protect the Project's intellectual property

---

### Q: Can I negotiate special terms of the CLA?

**A:** **Generally, no.** The CLA is standardized for all contributors to ensure fairness and consistency.

**Exception:** If you're making a very large contribution (e.g., a major feature, a new component, a company partnership), you may discuss alternative terms with the maintainers. Contact them directly.

## Maintainer Questions

### Q: How do I verify a contributor has signed?

**A:** The CLAassistant bot automatically posts a status comment on PRs. Look for:

- ✅ **"CLA Signed"** — Contributor has signed; OK to merge (pending other checks)
- ❌ **"CLA Not Signed"** — Contributor has not signed; block merge

The PR status check will also reflect this (if branch protection is enabled).

---

### Q: Can I whitelist maintainers to skip CLA signing?

**A:** Yes, CLAassistant supports whitelisting GitHub usernames to pre-approve without signing. This is optional and typically used for:

- Core team members (to reduce friction on rapid iterations)
- Bots (to avoid signing requirements for automated contributions)

Configure via CLAassistant's GitHub App settings.

---

### Q: What if a contributor refuses to sign?

**A:** You cannot merge their PR without a CLA signature (if branch protection is enabled). Options:

1. **Explain the policy** — Share this FAQ and the CLA document
2. **Clarify misconceptions** — Address specific concerns (e.g., copyright retention)
3. **Refer to maintainers** — If the contributor has unique circumstances, maintainers can discuss

If the contributor still refuses, the PR cannot be merged.
